About

This is an effort to capture how we think technology will evolve in the next 10 years. Everyone is invited to submit their technology related predictions. These predictions will be safely stored and kept secret. In December 2031 all predictions will be made public for everyone to enjoy.
In December 2031 everything submitted in the form above will be published publicly. Everyone will be able to see all submission. This includes the author's name if entered. We reserve the right to not publish submissions that are off topic.
You can't use this website.

Storing your digital contact details would be a pain. Technology evolves, email addresses change and storing secret personal information would require adequate measures. I don't want to commit to those responsibilities for a period of 10 years. That is why you are not able to submit your email address to a mailing list.

As an alternative you could use FutureMe to set a reminder.

There won't be a classic metal-box burried at some classified location. We want to keep control and ensure nobody accidentally finds it, therefore we will be making copies and distributing it among people we trust. Virtual copies will be stored in the cloud, on bare metal and offline (usb sticks). Also everything will be printed and sealed in envelops.
All valid submissions will be published in the public domain. It will be free for use by anyone for any purpose without restriction. By submitting the form you accept that all entered content can be released in the public domain as is. We reserve the right to not publish submissions that are off topic.
Assuming there will be a few hundred predictions, we will manually remove off-topic submissions. In case many more predictions are entered we will have to reside to the technology of the future to apply off-topic filtering. That's a problem to solve in november 2031 ;)
Anything that says something about how technology will be (or will change) in the future is on-topic. Predictions about how society will interact with technology are considered on-topic as well, as long as the (changing) technology is she main subject. Also, predictions are considered off-topic if they seem to be written with the intention to offend or use overly aggressive language.
The frontend is written in plain html, javascript and css without any fancy libraries because I don't want to worry about keeping dependencies up to date for over a period of 10 years. The site is served from an S3 bucket using CloudFront CDN. The backend is a serverless application using Lambda an DynamoDB running on AWS. The code that powers the website and infrastructure is hosted on GitHub.
